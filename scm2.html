Supply Chain Management 2.0

transformations within supply chains themselves and the development

Project Overview

The management of warehouses is crucial for a company's supply chain, as they serve as a central hub for the inflow and outflow of items, making effective warehouse management essential for optimizing the overall supply chain process.

Problem Statement:

The current mobile app is built using Supply Chain Management 1.0 practices.

  • Limited real-time visibility.
  • Inefficient manual processes.
  • Siloed data and systems.
  • Limited collaboration between partners.
  • Inflexible systems unable to adapt to changes in the marketplace.
  • Small screen

Goals & Metrics

The new app optimizes and automates supply chain processes, leading to increased efficiency, reduced costs, improved visibility, and greater agility in responding to marketplace changes.

  • Tasks driven
  • Real-time data updating
  • Scalable and can integrate with IoT, AI, and blockchain
  • Hardware - iPad mini

Data Inputing Principles for SCM2.0

To help warehouse associates work efficiently and accurately, clear UX principles for data inputting are crucial. These principles ensure that data is entered consistently and accurately to efficiently manage inventory and track the movement of goods.

  • Tap more, type less:
    Tapping is a simple and efficient method for users to input data. However, if there are many options available, it can become challenging to organize them effectively and efficiently.
  • Scanner is way faster:
    When inputting a large number of QR codes, using a scanner is significantly faster than using a camera.
  • Numeric keyboard:
    If only numbers are required, slide up the numeric keyboard.
  • Easy copy & paste:
    After user copied data, a "paste" button will be added to the next input.
  •     Voice to text    
    Due to limitations with accuracy and environmental factors, we have decided to drop the idea of using voice-to-text for data inputting.

Methods For Different Data Types

To optimize the data inputting process in a warehouse management system, it's important to select the most efficient and accessible method based on the type of data and user experience. This approach can lead to improved efficiency and accuracy, ultimately resulting in better warehouse management.

Design

user flow, wireframes, prototype and UI

User Flow

The SCM2.0 app is a task-driven application focused on functionality and usability. It streamlines workflows and increases efficiency by providing users with tools to manage projects, track progress, and collaborate with team members.

Design for iPad Mini

Compare to mobile, the larger size of the iPad Mini is ideal for warehouse associates, as it allows for easier viewing and navigation of data, more comfortable use over long periods of time, and more precise data input.

Basic Layouts

To optimize the app's display and make the most of the available screen space, the app is fixed to a horizontal view to ensure that the maximum width. This can improve the readability of the app's interface and make it easier for users to navigate through the various features and functions.

Wireframe & Prototype

During the wireframing process, each module of the app will be addressed individually, such as Search & Filter, Create Task, Group Tasks, Picking, Replenishment, Movement, Receiving, and Putaway...
Once each module is completed, meetings will be arranged to showcase the wireframes to different teams and departments. This approach ensures that each module is fully developed and tested before moving on to the next, and provides opportunities for feedback and collaboration throughout the development process.
